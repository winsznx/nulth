The pitch, in one line

Money moves only when it proves it obeyed rules the chain never sees.

Nulth is a Stellar account layer where every payment carries a zero-knowledge proof that it obeys a private policy — spend caps, allowlists, and compliance limits — without publishing those rules on-chain.

Nulth01
The problem

On-chain money has no private control layer.

Public policies
Your strategy leaks

Caps, allowlists, and approvers written on-chain are readable by competitors and attackers. For a treasury, the rules are the strategy.

Off-chain limits
Trust comes back

Enforcing limits in a backend reintroduces the trusted operator you were trying to remove — and the single point of failure with it.

AI agents
Filters get jailbroken

When agents hold wallets, a prompt filter can be talked past. The spending limit has to be structural — enforced below the model.

Nulth02
Why now

Stablecoin finance is going institutional — but controls are still built for humans.

  • ›Stablecoin treasuries are going institutional. Balances now demand real controls — caps, approvals, and audit trails.
  • ›Agentic payments are emerging. Software is starting to pay on its own; it needs a spending limit it cannot exceed.
  • ›Protocol 26 makes it viable. Native BN254 and Poseidon in Soroban make on-chain proof verification practical — a per-payment proof costs a constant 8.537% of the compute ceiling.
  • ›Stellar rewards real utility. The ecosystem values on-chain activity — new accounts, USDC volume, verifier calls — and Nulth drives all three.
Nulth03
The solution

Proof-authorized accounts.

Nulth removes the Ed25519 spending key from the payment path. A payment clears only if the browser produces a Groth16 proof that the amount is under the cap and the destination is on the allowlist. The proof is the signature — the account verifies it natively and authorizes itself.
